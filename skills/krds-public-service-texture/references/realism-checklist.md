# Real-Service Texture Checklist

Use this checklist when a public-service screen still feels like a design-system demo.

## Identity and Ownership
- Is the service or institution name specific rather than generic?
- Is the operating department, contact path, or owner visible where needed?
- Is there an update date, source label, or responsible-party signal?

## Operational Density
- Does the page show practical supporting information beyond the main CTA?
- Are there notices, schedules, requirements, attachments, counts, or support links?
- Is the first screen organized around service use instead of empty whitespace?

## Lists and Detail Pages
- Does the list include counts, dates, categories, or important-item treatment?
- Does the detail page expose attachments, owner info, and a return path?
- Is metadata dense enough to feel operational but still readable?

## Forms and Status
- Does the form show preparation requirements or supporting guidance?
- Is there a believable help, FAQ, or contact path near the form?
- Does the complete or status screen tell the user what happens next?

## Tone
- Does the copy sound administrative and useful instead of promotional?
- Are headings, labels, and buttons concrete?
- Does the page avoid startup-style claims and abstract KPI marketing?

## Layout Feel
- Is the layout mixed enough to feel like a live service rather than a repeated card demo?
- Are compact utility blocks present around the main content?
- Does the screen still respect KRDS trust markers and accessibility?
