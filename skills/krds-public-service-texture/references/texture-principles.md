# Public-Service Texture Principles

Use this file to understand what makes a KRDS-aligned screen feel like a real public institution service instead of a clean demo.

## 1. Texture Comes From Operations

The biggest difference between a guideline demo and a live public service is operational evidence.

Typical signals:
- managing department or center
- contact channel
- update date
- policy or FAQ link
- requirement summary
- attachments
- notice count, schedule, or processing status

If the page only shows a title and a neat set of cards, it will feel generic.

## 2. Texture Comes From Mixed Density

Real public-service pages mix dense and light blocks.

Common combinations:
- large service-entry block next to compact notice summaries
- board list above a dense filter toolbar
- long-form detail content followed by a compact attachment area
- form main column with smaller help, requirement, and contact side blocks

Do not force every section into identical cards.

## 3. Texture Comes From Accountability

Live public services usually answer these questions implicitly:
- Who runs this service?
- Where do I ask for help?
- When was this updated?
- What happens after I submit?
- Which document do I download?

If the page does not answer them, it feels incomplete even when the layout is tidy.

## 4. Texture Comes From Administrative Copy

Prefer copy that sounds procedural:
- "신청 대상과 제출 서류를 확인해 주세요."
- "처리 결과는 마이페이지와 문자로 안내합니다."
- "담당 부서와 문의처는 하단 안내를 참고해 주세요."

Avoid copy that sounds promotional:
- "더 나은 미래를 위한 혁신 플랫폼"
- "간편하고 스마트한 경험을 제공합니다"

## 5. Texture Still Needs KRDS Discipline

This skill does not replace KRDS compliance.

Keep:
- official banner and identifier usage conditions
- accessibility and keyboard focus
- current KRDS breadcrumb, footer, help-panel, and step-flow rules
- restrained color usage

Use this skill only after the official KRDS baseline is already safe.

## 6. Typical Causes of the "Mockup" Feeling

Screens usually feel fake when they have:
- equal-height cards everywhere
- filler copy instead of public-service microcopy
- no owner, date, or contact information
- no attachments or linked supporting information
- no total counts, filters, or metadata on list pages
- no next-step explanation after an action
- too much whitespace and too little operational context
